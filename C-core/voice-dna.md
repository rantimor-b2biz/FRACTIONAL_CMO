# Voice DNA

> How Ran Timor sounds - executive-level, crisp, direct, and slightly contrarian. Like a senior advisor who's built this before and knows what actually matters.

---

## Voice Overview

**In one sentence:** Ran Timor sounds like an experienced CMO who speaks with boardroom clarity - strategic but grounded, insightful without being fluffy, and willing to challenge conventional marketing wisdom when it doesn't serve B2B tech startups.

**Not a consultant with a deck. A leader with scar tissue.**

---

## Voice Attributes

### 1. Executive-Level but Accessible

**What this means:**
- Speaks at the CEO/Board level - strategic thinking, commercial focus
- Uses clear language that anyone in the room can understand
- No unnecessary jargon, but comfortable with technical concepts
- Focused on **outcomes** and **ROI**, not just activities

**What this doesn't mean:**
- Not overly formal or stiff
- Not academic or theoretical
- Not trying to sound smart with buzzwords
- Not talking down to anyone

**Examples:**
```
✅ "Positioning beats performance marketing. Fix the foundation before you spend on ads."
✅ "Your sales team shouldn't need 30 minutes to explain what you do."
❌ "We leverage synergistic capabilities to optimize your go-to-market infrastructure."
❌ "Our best-in-class solutions revolutionize the enterprise landscape."
```

---

### 2. Direct and Crisp

**What this means:**
- Gets to the point quickly
- Short sentences that land
- No fluff, no filler
- Respects the reader's time

**What this doesn't mean:**
- Not rude or abrupt
- Not oversimplifying complex topics
- Not leaving out necessary context

**Examples:**
```
✅ "AI amplifies what you feed it. If your positioning is unclear, AI just makes the confusion faster."
✅ "You don't need more activity. You need clarity."
❌ "It's really important to understand that in today's rapidly evolving marketplace..."
❌ "I believe that organizations should consider the possibility that..."
```

---

### 3. Slightly Contrarian

**What this means:**
- Willing to challenge common startup marketing advice
- Points out when "best practices" don't apply to B2B tech
- Takes a position, doesn't hedge everything
- Backed by experience, not just opinions

**What this doesn't mean:**
- Not contrarian for the sake of attention
- Not dismissive of other approaches
- Not claiming to have all the answers
- Not arrogant or condescending

**Examples:**
```
✅ "Most B2B startups over-invest in tactics and under-invest in positioning."
✅ "Your ICP isn't 'anyone who needs X.' That's a recipe for scattered GTM."
✅ "Speed is overrated when you're running in the wrong direction."
❌ "Everyone else is wrong. Here's the one true way."
❌ "Traditional marketing is dead."
```

---

### 4. Insight-Driven

**What this means:**
- Every piece of content teaches something actionable
- Backed by real experience (not just theory)
- Frameworks and mental models that readers can apply
- Specific examples, not vague claims

**What this doesn't mean:**
- Not trying to sound profound without substance
- Not hiding behind complexity
- Not just sharing "hot takes" without value
- Not repeating generic advice

**Examples:**
```
✅ "When your pitch deck changes after every sales meeting, that's a positioning problem, not a sales problem."
✅ "If your ICP is broader than your sales team can handle, you're setting up for inefficiency."
❌ "Thought leadership is key to success."
❌ "Marketing is changing faster than ever."
```

---

### 5. Grounded in Experience

**What this means:**
- Speaks from 15+ years of building marketing functions
- References real situations (anonymized when needed)
- Acknowledges what's hard, not just what's possible
- Practical over aspirational

**What this doesn't mean:**
- Not telling war stories for the sake of it
- Not name-dropping or credentials-flexing
- Not "in my experience" as a crutch
- Not dismissing what works for others

**Examples:**
```
✅ "I've built this function three times. Here's what I'd do differently now."
✅ "At Tactile Mobility, we repositioned from tech feature to business outcome. Sales cycles shortened by 40%."
❌ "As a world-renowned expert with 15 years of experience..."
❌ "Back in my day, we didn't have these problems."
```

## Language Choices

### Words I Use
- **Clarity, positioning, GTM, alignment, strategic, judgment**
- **Founders, CEOs, enterprise, startups, B2B, complex products**
- **Focused, lean, practical, grounded, executive-level**
- **ROI, pipeline, sales cycle, buying process, decision-makers**

### Words I Avoid
- **Synergy, disruptive, game-changer, revolutionize, cutting-edge**
- **Best-in-class, world-class, industry-leading** (without proof)
- **Leverage, optimize, maximize** (corporate jargon)
- **Amazing, incredible, unbelievable** (hype words)
- **Guaranteed, proven, foolproof** (empty promises)

### Phrases That Sound Like Me
```
"Positioning beats performance marketing."

"AI amplifies confusion when your messaging isn't clear."

"Your ICP definition determines your GTM efficiency."

"Clarity before speed. Direction before velocity."

"You don't need a full-time CMO. You need CMO-level thinking."

"If sales needs 30 minutes to explain value, positioning isn't done."
```

### Phrases That Don't Sound Like Me
```
"Revolutionize your go-to-market with our proven methodology!"
→ Instead: "Clear positioning makes GTM actually work."

"Leverage AI to optimize synergistic marketing outcomes!"
→ Instead: "Use AI after you fix your messaging."

"We're disrupting the enterprise SaaS landscape!"
→ Instead: "Most B2B startups get positioning wrong. Here's how to fix it."

"Our best-in-class framework guarantees 10x pipeline growth!"
→ Instead: "This framework helped us cut sales cycles by 40%."
```

## Writing Style Rules

### Sentence Length
- **Short sentences for impact** - One idea, one punch.
- **Longer sentences for explanation** - When context is needed.
- **Mix them for rhythm** - Short. Then longer. Then short again.

**Example:**
```
Your positioning is unclear.

That's not a judgment - it's a pattern I see across B2B tech startups at Seed to Series B. The product works, the founding team is strong, and early customers validate the need. But messaging is inconsistent, sales cycles are too long, and everyone explains the value differently.

Fix positioning first.
```

### Paragraph Length
- **1-3 sentences per paragraph** (especially on LinkedIn)
- **One idea per paragraph**
- **White space is strategic** - makes content scannable

### Formatting
- **Bold for emphasis** - not underline, not ALL CAPS
- **Bullets for lists** - easier to scan than paragraphs
- **Numbers for frameworks** - "3 reasons" > "several reasons"
- **Line breaks create rhythm** - especially in LinkedIn posts

### Punctuation
- **Periods for authority** - clear statements.
- **Em dashes sparingly** - only for brief asides in short-form (LinkedIn posts). Avoid in long-form articles.
- **No exclamation marks** - almost never. (Excitement reads as hype.)
- **Question marks sparingly** - for engagement, not rhetorical fluff.

## Voice Examples

### Good Example (LinkedIn Post)
```
AI speeds up the wrong things.

I see B2B startups rushing to "AI-enable" their marketing before they've nailed positioning.

The result?

Faster confusion.

If your ICP is too broad, AI will help you target more of the wrong people.
If your messaging is generic, AI will help you write more generic content.
If your sales deck changes every meeting, AI will just iterate faster on something broken.

Fix the foundation first.

Then let AI amplify what works.
```

**Why it works:**
- Opens with a contrarian hook
- Short, punchy sentences
- Clear structure with white space
- Insight-driven (not just opinion)
- Actionable takeaway

### Bad Example (Corporate Fluff)
```
At our organization, we believe in leveraging cutting-edge AI technologies to revolutionize go-to-market strategies and optimize enterprise marketing outcomes. Our best-in-class framework synergizes positioning excellence with data-driven insights to deliver world-class results that maximize pipeline generation and accelerate revenue growth across all stakeholder touchpoints.
```

**What's wrong:**
- Wall of buzzwords
- No clear insight
- Sounds like everyone else
- Not actionable
- Tries too hard to sound impressive

---

### Good Example (Thought Leadership Article Opening)
```
# Positioning Before Performance Marketing

Most B2B tech startups get the sequence wrong.

They raise a Series A, hire a Head of Marketing, and immediately spin up performance campaigns - LinkedIn ads, content syndication, webinars, ABM tools.

Three months later, CAC is too high, MQLs don't convert, and the CEO questions whether marketing is working.

The problem isn't execution. It's that you skipped the foundation.

Before you spend on performance marketing, you need positioning clarity. Here's why - and how to fix it.
```

**Why it works:**
- Clear thesis up front
- Paints a familiar problem
- Credible (not preachy)
- Sets up a framework
- Reader knows what's coming

## The "Sound Like Ran" Test

Before publishing, ask:

1. **Would a CEO read this and think "this person gets it"?**
2. **Is it specific enough to be useful, not vague advice?**
3. **Does it sound like a human who's done this, not a consultant who studied it?**
4. **Would I say this in a boardroom conversation?**
5. **Is it free of buzzwords and marketing jargon?**

If any answer is "no," revise.

## Visual Voice

Tone extends to visual presentation:

### LinkedIn Posts
- **Short lines** - 1-2 sentences per line
- **Strategic white space** - breathing room between ideas
- **Bold sparingly** - for key phrases only
- **Emojis rarely** - only when it adds clarity (not decoration)
- **No ALL CAPS** - except acronyms (GTM, ICP, CMO)

### Articles
- **Clear headers** - guide the reader
- **Subheaders for structure** - framework sections
- **Bulleted insights** - easier to scan
- **Real examples** - anonymized case studies when relevant

### Comments & Engagement
- **Add value, not sales pitches**
- **Ask genuine questions**
- **Build on others' ideas**
- **Disagree respectfully when needed**
