# LinkedIn Post Draft

People are getting tired of AI.

Not the technology. The output.

I'm seeing it in engagement metrics, in DMs, in deal cycles.

A prospect reads a polished LinkedIn post. It hits all the right beats. The framework is clever. The copy is tight.

Then they realize: it probably came from ChatGPT.

So they scroll past.

Here's what's happening:

AI got really good at sounding human. But it got bad at sounding *true*.

There's no scar tissue in it. No judgment call. No moment where the writer had to be wrong, then learned something.

AI can copy the *form* of authenticity. It can't replicate the *substance*.

And the market is starting to notice.

---

**So how do you sound human in an AI-saturated market?**

Be specific.

Not "B2B startups struggle with positioning" (AI wrote that).
But "I worked with a Series A founder who had 12 enterprise customers. Churn hit 40% the moment they tried to scale. Their sales deck was built for land-grab deals, not enterprise buying" (you lived that).

Have a real point of view.

Not "positioning is important for GTM success" (everyone says that).
But "Positioning before performance marketing. I've seen it destroy two companies and save three others" (you've made a judgment call).

Show the work.

Not "we grew pipeline 200%."
But "We grew pipeline 200%. But our ICP was still too broad. It took narrowing our focus and saying no to revenue opportunities. That's what actually moved the needle" (you can defend this).

---

**That's authenticity.**

It's not better writing. It's clearer thinking.

And AI can't do that for you — because AI doesn't have skin in the game.

You do.

The competitive advantage right now isn't a better AI tool.

It's a clearer head and the courage to say what you actually believe.
