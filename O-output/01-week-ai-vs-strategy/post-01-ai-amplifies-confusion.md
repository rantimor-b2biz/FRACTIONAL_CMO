## The Post

Most B2B startups are using AI to scale confusion faster.

In 2026, AI can draft content in seconds.
Write emails.
Repurpose posts.
Optimize campaigns.

But here's what I see across early-stage companies:

If your ICP is too broad, AI helps you target more of the wrong people.
If your messaging is inconsistent, AI produces more inconsistent content.
If your positioning is unclear, AI amplifies that confusion across every channel.

AI is not the problem.

It is a leverage upgrade, not a strategy upgrade.

The startups winning with AI have one thing in common:
They fixed positioning first.

At Tactile Mobility, we repositioned before we scaled campaigns.
Sales cycles dropped from 12 months to 7.

Same product.
Clearer message.
AI accelerated what already worked.

The question is not "Are we using AI?"
The question is "What are we using AI to amplify?"

Curious to hear from founders and marketing leaders:
What did you fix before you let AI accelerate execution?
