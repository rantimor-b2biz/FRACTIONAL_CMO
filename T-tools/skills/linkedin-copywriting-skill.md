## The Ran's Voice Foundation

Before using any template, remember these voice principles:

**Executive level** — Speak at CEO/board level, not junior marketer level
**Direct and crisp** — Short sentences. One idea = one punch.
**Slightly contrarian** — Challenge conventional wisdom (backed by experience)
**Specific, not vague** — Use numbers, real situations, actual outcomes
**No buzzwords** — Avoid: synergy, disruptive, game-changer, leverage, optimize
**Grounded** — "I've seen this," "At [company]," "What I'm noticing"

## Post Templates

### Template 1: Contrarian Take (20-30% of posts)

**Best for:** Challenging conventional wisdom, positioning unique perspective

**Structure:**
```
[HOOK] — Short contrarian statement that challenges assumption

[PROBLEM STATEMENT] — Why the conventional wisdom is wrong

[EXPERIENCE] — Specific example of what you've seen

[INSIGHT] — What actually works instead

[CLOSING THOUGHT] — Why this matters
```

**Example (from authenticity post):**

```
People are getting tired of AI.

Not the technology. The output.

I'm seeing it in engagement metrics, in DMs, in deal cycles.

A prospect reads a polished LinkedIn post. It hits all the right beats.
The framework is clever. The copy is tight.

Then they realize: it probably came from ChatGPT.

So they scroll past.

Here's what's happening: AI got really good at sounding human.
But it got bad at sounding *true*.

There's no scar tissue in it. No judgment call.
No moment where the writer had to be wrong, then learned something.

AI can copy the *form* of authenticity. It can't replicate the *substance*.

And the market is starting to notice.
```

**Length:** 120-180 words
**Format:** Short lines for rhythm, white space is strategic
**Engagement driver:** Specific observation + contrarian angle

### Template 2: Framework Post (20-30% of posts)

**Best for:** Teaching something, providing structure, solving a problem

**Structure:**
```
[HOOK] — The problem or insight

[SETUP] — Why this matters

[FRAMEWORK] — 3-5 numbered/bulleted items
- Item 1 (short description)
- Item 2 (short description)
- Item 3 (short description)

[APPLICATION] — How to use it

[CLOSING] — Why founders should care
```

**Example (authenticity post framework section):**

```
**So how do you sound human in an AI-saturated market?**

Be specific.

Not "B2B startups struggle with positioning" (AI wrote that).
But "I worked with a Series A founder who had 12 enterprise customers.
Churn hit 40% the moment they tried to scale. Their sales deck was
built for land-grab deals, not enterprise buying" (you lived that).

Have a real point of view.

Not "positioning is important for GTM success" (everyone says that).
But "Positioning before performance marketing. I've seen it destroy
two companies and save three others" (you've made a judgment call).

Show the work.

Not "we grew pipeline 200%."
But "We grew pipeline 200%. But our ICP was still too broad.
It took narrowing our focus and saying no to revenue opportunities.
That's what actually moved the needle" (you can defend this).
```

**Length:** 150-220 words
**Format:** Bold for framework items, specific examples, clear structure
**Engagement driver:** Teaches something actionable + specific examples

### Template 3: Experience/Story Post (15-20% of posts)

**Best for:** Sharing a specific moment that taught you something

**Structure:**
```
[HOOK] — The situation or moment

[STORY] — What happened (specific details matter)

[CONSEQUENCE] — What was the result/what did you learn

[INSIGHT] — What this teaches about B2B marketing/GTM

[QUESTION or INVITATION] — Close with engagement
```

**Example structure:**

```
We had a crisis at [Company] last quarter.

[Specific situation - numbers, details, what went wrong]

[What happened next - decision made, action taken]

The result: [specific outcome with metrics]

What this taught me: [insight that applies broadly to founders]

[Question for others in comments]
```

**Length:** 140-200 words
**Format:** Narrative flow, short paragraphs, specific numbers
**Engagement driver:** Relatable story + universal lesson + question

### Template 4: Hot Take / Debate Post (10-15% of posts)

**Best for:** Jumping into trending conversations with a contrarian angle

**Structure:**
```
[DIRECT STATEMENT] — Your contrarian take (no softening)

[ACKNOWLEDGMENT] — What the other side is saying (credibility)

[BUT] — Why they're missing something

[EVIDENCE] — What you've actually seen

[CLOSING] — What founders should do differently
```

**Example:**

```
Most advice on [topic] is wrong.

I get why people say [conventional wisdom]. It sounds smart.

But what I've seen across 50+ founder conversations is [different reality].

Example: [specific situation showing why conventional wisdom fails]

The shift: [what actually works]

If you're [specific founder scenario], here's what I'd focus on first: [action]
```

**Length:** 120-160 words
**Format:** Short, punchy sentences, clear position
**Engagement driver:** Boldly taking a position + inviting debate

---

### Template 5: Question / Diagnosis Post (5-10% of posts)

**Best for:** Identifying a problem founders face, positioning yourself as expert

**Structure:**
```
[QUESTION] — Diagnostic question about positioning/GTM

[OBSERVATION] — What you're seeing in market

[SECOND QUESTION] — Deeper follow-up

[INSIGHT] — Here's what usually goes wrong

[INVITATION] — Comment with your version
```

**Example:**

```
When your pitch deck changes after every sales meeting, that's a positioning problem, not a sales problem.

I see this pattern constantly:

Sales team tweaks deck for each prospect.
Founder rewrites positioning after every objection.
Marketing messaging doesn't match sales pitch.

The result?

Sales cycles get longer. Close rate stays low. Team gets frustrated.

Here's what I'd diagnose first:

Is your ICP actually clear? Or are you chasing "anyone who needs X"?

If it's the second one, everything downstream gets harder.

What's your version? What positioning problem are you wrestling with right now?
```

**Length:** 130-180 words
**Format:** Questions + observations + invitation
**Engagement driver:** Diagnostic questions + invitation to share problems

## Hook Patterns That Work

These openings have driven high engagement for Ran:

### Hook Type 1: Contrarian Observation
```
"People are getting tired of [conventional thing]."

"Most [founder type] get [decision] wrong."

"Your [process] is actually a [problem type] problem."

"[Common belief] is wrong."
```

**Performance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ High engagement on ICP personas

### Hook Type 2: Specific Problem
```
"When your [symptom], that's a [root cause] problem."

"I see this pattern constantly: [situation]."

"You don't need [what everyone thinks]. You need [what actually matters]."

"[Specific industry/scenario] is making one critical mistake."
```

**Performance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Strong engagement, high relevance to ICP

### Hook Type 3: Bold Statement
```
"AI doesn't solve [X]. It amplifies [Y]."

"Your [process] should happen before [common thing]."

"[Conventional wisdom] is a trap."

"Positioning beats [alternative]."
```

**Performance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ High shares, good debate in comments

### Hook Type 4: Question
```
"Why does [specific situation] happen?"

"What's your version of [problem]?"

"Have you noticed [observation] in your company?"
```

**Performance:** ⭐⭐⭐ Good for engagement (people answer), lower viral potential

### Hook Type 5: Specific Number
```
"I worked with [number] founders facing [problem]."

"We cut [metric] by [number]% by doing one thing differently."

"[Percentage]% of B2B startups [mistake]."
```

**Performance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High credibility, specific engagement

## Format & Structure Rules

### Sentence Length (Critical)
- **Hook sentence:** 5-12 words (punchy)
- **Explanation sentences:** 10-20 words (clear)
- **Context sentences:** 20-30 words (nuance)
- **Mix actively** — Short. Then longer. Then short again.

**Example:**
```
Your positioning is unclear.

That's not a judgment — it's a pattern I see across B2B tech startups
at Seed to Series B. The product works, the founding team is strong,
and early customers validate the need.

But messaging is inconsistent.
```

### Paragraph Length
- **LinkedIn posts:** 1-3 sentences per paragraph
- **One idea per paragraph**
- **White space is strategic** — Use it to control rhythm
- **Avoid walls of text** — If it looks dense, it won't get read

### Punctuation
- **Periods for authority** — "Clear statements."
- **Em dashes for pauses** — "Like this — when adding context."
- **NO exclamation marks** — Almost never (excitement reads as hype)
- **Question marks sparingly** — For engagement, not rhetorical fluff

### Formatting
- **Bold for emphasis** — Not underline, not ALL CAPS
- **Bullets for lists** — Easier to scan
- **Line breaks create rhythm** — Use intentionally
- **NO emojis** (Ran's style is crisp, professional)

## What NOT to Do

### ❌ Don't: Generic Advice
```
"Here are 5 ways to improve positioning."
→ Vague, not specific, every expert says this
```

### ❌ Don't: Hype Language
```
"This will REVOLUTIONIZE your go-to-market! 🚀"
→ Sounds like a pitch, not insight
```

### ❌ Don't: Corporate Speak
```
"We leverage synergistic capabilities to optimize outcomes."
→ Exactly what Ran doesn't sound like
```

### ❌ Don't: Self-Promotion
```
"Great post! We help with this. DM me 👇"
→ Comments should add value, not sell
```

### ❌ Don't: Vague Claims
```
"Positioning is key to success."
→ True but useless. Show what actually happens
```

## Workflow: Using This Skill

**Before writing a post:**

1. **Know your angle** — What's the contrarian take? What's the insight?
2. **Pick a template** — Which format fits best?
3. **Write the hook first** — Does it stop the scroll?
4. **Add specific examples** — Numbers, situations, outcomes
5. **Check the rhythm** — Short. Longer. Short again.
6. **Remove buzzwords** — Anything that sounds corporate?
7. **Read as Ran** — Would he say this in a boardroom?
8. **Format for LinkedIn** — White space, line breaks, bold for emphasis

**Then:** Pass to Gatekeeper for quality review
