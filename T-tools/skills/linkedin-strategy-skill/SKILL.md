# LinkedIn Strategy Skill

Executive-level thought leadership for B2B tech audience.

## Instructions

### Step 1: Understand the Content Type
Determine post type:
- **Contrarian insight**: Challenge common wisdom
- **Bold claim**: Strong position backed by experience
- **Story-driven**: Real scenario from field
- **Strategic framework**: Clear mental model

### Step 2: Write the Post

Use this structure:
```
[Hook: Contrarian/Bold statement - 1-2 sentences]

[Context: Why this matters - 2-3 sentences]

[Insight: Strategic take - 3-4 sentences]

[Application: What to do - 2-3 sentences]

[CTA: Question for engagement - 1 sentence]
```

**Length:** 150-300 words (sweet spot for B2B engagement)

See [references/post-formulas.md](references/post-formulas.md) for detailed examples.

### Step 3: Apply Voice Guidelines

✅ **Do:**
- Executive voice: "AI amplifies unclear positioning."
- Specific examples: "Sales cycles dropped from 12 months to 7"
- Short lines: 1-2 sentences per paragraph
- White space for mobile scanning

❌ **Don't:**
- Marketing fluff: "Revolutionize your go-to-market with AI! 🚀"
- Vague claims: "Sales improved significantly"
- Dense paragraphs
- Buzzwords without substance

See [references/voice-guidelines.md](references/voice-guidelines.md) for complete rules.

### Step 4: Quality Check
Before delivering:
- [ ] Executive-level voice (not marketing fluff)?
- [ ] No buzzwords without substance?
- [ ] Specific examples or numbers included?
- [ ] Formatted for mobile (short lines, white space)?
- [ ] CTA invites meaningful engagement?
- [ ] 150-300 words?
- [ ] One clear insight (not 5 ideas)?

## Common Issues

**Issue:** Post sounds too sales-y
**Solution:** Lead with insight, not pitch. Share strategic thinking first. Value before ask.

**Issue:** Too long or dense
**Solution:** Break into 1-2 sentence paragraphs. Add white space. Cut to 300 words max.

**Issue:** Vague or generic
**Solution:** Add specific numbers, names, timelines. "Series B SaaS CEO" not "a client"

## Examples

**Example 1: Contrarian Hook**
User says: "Write about AI in marketing"

Actions:
1. Identify contrarian angle: AI speeds up wrong things
2. Open with bold statement
3. Provide context why this matters
4. Share strategic insight from experience
5. End with actionable takeaway + engagement question

Result: 250-word post with clear POV and engagement hook
