# LinkedIn Post Formulas

## Standard Post Structure

```
[Hook: 1-2 sentences]

[Context: 2-3 sentences]

[Insight: 3-4 sentences]

[Application: 2-3 sentences]

[CTA: 1 sentence with question]
```

**Total:** 150-300 words

---

## Formula 1: Contrarian Insight

**Use when:** You want to challenge common wisdom

**Structure:**
```
[Contrarian statement that surprises]

[Why most people get this wrong]

[What you've learned from experience]

[What to do instead]

[Engagement question]
```

**Example:**
```
AI speeds up the wrong things.

Most B2B companies use AI to create more content.
More blog posts. More social posts. More everything.

But content volume isn't the problem.
Clarity is.

When your positioning is unclear, AI just helps you confuse people faster.
Your ICP is too broad. Your messaging tries to be everything to everyone.

AI can't fix that.

Here's what works:
→ Get positioning right first
→ Narrow your ICP ruthlessly
→ Then use AI to amplify what's already clear

What's one area where you're using AI to speed up the wrong work?
```

## Formula 2: Framework/Mental Model

**Use when:** You have a clear strategic framework to share

**Structure:**
```
[Problem statement]

[The framework - 3-4 clear points]

[Why this matters]

[How to apply it]

[Question for input]
```

**Example:**
```
Most Series B companies skip the hardest marketing work.

They jump straight to tactics:
→ Hire a demand gen lead
→ Scale paid ads
→ Build content engine

But they haven't done the foundational work:
→ Sharp positioning
→ Narrow ICP
→ Clear value prop
→ Messaging that lands

You can't scale what isn't clear.

The hard work is boring:
Positioning workshops. Customer interviews. Messaging iterations.

But this work is what makes everything else easier.

What's the "boring" work you're tempted to skip?
```

## Formula 3: Story-Driven Insight

**Use when:** You have a real scenario that illustrates a point

**Structure:**
```
[Open with specific story]

[What happened]

[The insight from this]

[The broader pattern]

[Application + question]
```

**Example:**
```
"Should we hire a CMO or a VP Demand Gen?"

A Series B founder asked me this yesterday.
$15M ARR. Growing fast. Zero marketing leadership.

Here's what I asked back:
"Do you have clear positioning?"

Silence.

"Do you know exactly who your ICP is?"

"We have a few different segments..."

That's the answer.

You need a CMO.

Demand gen scales what's already working.
A CMO figures out what should work in the first place.

Hire for strategy first, execution second.

What's your signal that it's time for strategic marketing leadership?
```

## Formula 4: Data-Driven Pattern

**Use when:** You have specific numbers or patterns from experience

**Structure:**
```
[Surprising data point or pattern]

[Why this happens]

[What it means]

[What to do about it]

[Question for engagement]
```

**Example:**
```
3 positioning workshops in Q1.
Same pattern every time.

The executive team thinks their ICP is:
"B2B companies that need [our solution]"

Way. Too. Broad.

After 3 hours of digging:
→ 80% of revenue comes from 2 specific segments
→ Those segments share 3-4 unique characteristics
→ The use cases are completely different

Actual ICP: Much narrower. Much more valuable.

The pattern:
Companies define ICP by who CAN buy.
Not who DOES buy and gets the most value.

One question that fixes this:
"Who gets so much value they'd pay 3x more?"

How do you define your ICP?
```

## Quick Tips for All Formulas

### Length
- **Minimum:** 150 words (too short feels incomplete)
- **Sweet spot:** 200-250 words
- **Maximum:** 300 words (attention drops after this)

### Paragraph Breaks
- 1-3 sentences max per paragraph
- Add blank line between each paragraph
- Use white space liberally

### Emphasis
- Bold for key points (use sparingly)
- → Arrows for lists
- Line breaks for impact

### Numbers and Specifics
- Always prefer specific over vague
- "12 months to 7 months" > "faster"
- "3 workshops" > "recently"
- "$15M ARR Series B" > "a client"

### Engagement
- End with a question that matters
- Make it specific to the topic
- Invite real input, not just "thoughts?"
