# Voice Guidelines - B2B Executive LinkedIn

## Core Principles

### 1. Executive Voice, Not Marketing Fluff
Speak like a strategic advisor, not a marketer pushing product.

**✅ Executive Voice:**
- "AI amplifies unclear positioning."
- "Your ICP is probably too broad."
- "Most Series B companies skip positioning work."

**❌ Marketing Fluff:**
- "Revolutionize your go-to-market with AI! 🚀"
- "Unlock unprecedented growth! 💡"
- "Transform your business today!"

### 2. One Clear Insight Per Post
Don't try to say 5 things. One idea, well delivered.

**✅ Focused:**
Post about ONE thing: why positioning matters before scaling ads

**❌ Scattered:**
Post touching positioning, ICP, messaging, sales enablement, and brand

### 3. Specific Over Vague
Give real numbers, timeframes, examples.

**✅ Specific:**
- "Sales cycles dropped from 12 months to 7"
- "Series B SaaS company, $15M ARR"
- "3 positioning workshops in Q1"

**❌ Vague:**
- "Sales improved significantly"
- "A client of mine"
- "Recently worked with a company"

### 4. Format for Mobile Reading
Most LinkedIn users read on mobile. Short lines, white space, easy scan.

**✅ Mobile-Friendly:**
```
AI speeds up the wrong things.

Most B2B companies use AI to create more content.
More blog posts. More social posts. More everything.

But content volume isn't the problem.
Clarity is.
```

**❌ Dense Wall of Text:**
```
AI speeds up the wrong things and most B2B companies use AI to create more content including more blog posts, more social posts, and more everything but content volume isn't actually the problem that needs solving because clarity is what matters most.
```

## Hook Formulas

### Formula 1: Contrarian Statement
Challenge common wisdom in your space.

**Examples:**
- "AI speeds up the wrong things."
- "Your ICP is too broad."
- "Positioning isn't brand work."

### Formula 2: Bold Claim
Make a strong statement you can back with experience.

**Examples:**
- "Most Series B companies skip the hardest work."
- "Clarity beats volume every time."
- "Your best prospects aren't reading your content."

### Formula 3: Story Opening
Start with a real scenario.

**Examples:**
- "Last week, a Series B CEO showed me their deck..."
- "Yesterday a founder asked me: 'Should we hire a CMO?'"
- "Three positioning workshops in. Same pattern every time."

## CTA Guidelines

End with a question that invites real engagement, not generic "thoughts?"

**✅ Good CTAs:**
- "What's the hardest part of positioning work for you?"
- "Where do you see this pattern in your market?"
- "What would you add to this framework?"

**❌ Weak CTAs:**
- "Thoughts?"
- "Agree?"
- "What do you think?"

## Tone Guidelines

- **Confident but not arrogant**
- **Helpful but not prescriptive**
- **Experienced but not preachy**
- **Direct but not harsh**

## Things to Avoid

❌ Excessive emojis (1-2 max, if any)
❌ Hashtag stuffing
❌ ALL CAPS for emphasis
❌ Clickbait language
❌ Overpromising results
❌ Generic business jargon
❌ Long paragraphs (3 lines max)
