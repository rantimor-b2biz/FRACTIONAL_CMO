## Core Principles

### Principle 1: Quality Metrics Over Vanity Metrics

Focus on metrics that drive business outcomes.

**Quality metrics (prioritize):**
- **Engagement rate** (comments + shares / impressions)
- **Comment quality** (from ICP personas vs. generic)
- **DMs triggered** (inbound interest from target audience)
- **Profile views from posts** (discovery signal)
- **Follower quality** (job titles match ICP)

**Vanity metrics (de-prioritize):**
- Total likes (without context)
- Follower count (without quality assessment)
- Impressions (without engagement)

**Why:** 1,000 likes from random people < 10 comments from Technical Founder Tom.

---

### Principle 2: Context Over Numbers

Numbers without context are meaningless.

**Bad analysis:**
```
Post got 150 likes.
```

**Good analysis:**
```
Post got 150 likes (2x average) with 18 comments.

Why it worked:
- Contrarian hook ("You are not 'too busy' to post")
- Direct challenge to founder assumption
- 3-stage framework (Silent → Reactive → Systematic)
- Posted Monday 9 AM (optimal time)

Who engaged:
- 12 comments from founders/CEOs (ICP match)
- 3 DMs asking about fractional CMO services

Recommendation:
- More contrarian angles challenging founder assumptions
- More maturity models/frameworks
- Continue Monday AM posting
```

---

### Principle 3: Actionable Insights

Every analysis must end with clear next actions.

**Not actionable:**
```
"Engagement is down this week."
```

**Actionable:**
```
"Engagement is down 30% this week.

Likely cause: Posted 2 generic posts instead of contrarian insights.

Next actions:
1. Return to contrarian angles ("Not X. It is Y." structure)
2. Add specific examples with numbers
3. Test posting Thursday 10 AM (data shows higher reach)
```

## Key Metrics to Track

### Post Performance

| Metric | What It Tells You | Target Benchmark |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Impressions** | How many people saw it | Growing week-over-week |
| **Engagement Rate** | How compelling it was | >3% (B2B thought leadership) |
| **Comments** | Quality of conversation | 10-30 per post (meaningful discussion) |
| **Shares** | Content worth spreading | 5-15 per post |
| **Click-through (if link)** | Interest in deeper content | >2% |
| **DMs triggered** | Direct business interest | 1-3 per week from ICP |

### Profile Performance

| Metric | What It Tells You | Target Benchmark |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Profile views** | Discovery from posts | 100-300 per week |
| **Search appearances** | SEO and relevance | Growing monthly |
| **Follower growth** | Audience building | 50-100 per month (quality) |
| **Connection requests** | Network expansion | 10-20 per week from ICP |

| **Company relevance** | Target market alignment | % from B2B tech startups |
| **Geography** | Market focus | US, Europe, Israel concentration |
| **Seniority** | Decision-maker reach | Director+ level engagement |

## Weekly Analytics Workflow

### Monday Morning (30 minutes)

**Review last week's performance:**

1. **Pull data for all posts** (views, likes, comments, shares, engagement rate)
2. **Identify top performer** (highest engagement rate + ICP comments)
3. **Identify underperformer** (lowest engagement, understand why)
4. **Review profile metrics** (views, followers, search appearances)
5. **Check qualitative signals** (DMs, connection requests from ICP)

**Output: Weekly Performance Report** (see template below)

---

### Monthly Deep Dive (90 minutes)

**Analyze trends and patterns:**

1. **Content themes:** Which topics drove best engagement?
2. **Format analysis:** What structures worked? (frameworks, stories, contrarian)
3. **Hook performance:** Which opening lines stopped the scroll?
4. **Posting schedule:** Best days and times?
5. **Audience growth:** Quality of new followers?
6. **Lead generation:** How many inquiries came from LinkedIn?

**Output: Monthly Analytics Report + Strategic Recommendations**

---

### Quarterly Review (2 hours)

**Strategic assessment:**

1. **Goal progress:** Are we hitting quarterly targets? (leads, visibility, thought leadership)
2. **Content strategy effectiveness:** Which themes worked? Which didn't?
3. **Audience development:** Are we reaching more of the right people?
4. **Competitive benchmarking:** How do we compare to similar thought leaders?
5. **Strategic pivots:** What should we change next quarter?

**Output: Quarterly Performance Review + Strategy Adjustments**

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Don't Do This:

**Mistake 1: Report numbers without context**
→ "150 likes" means nothing without comparison and insight.

**Mistake 2: Focus on vanity metrics**
→ Total likes < Engagement from ICP personas.

**Mistake 3: No actionable recommendations**
→ Every report must end with "Do this next."

**Mistake 4: Ignore qualitative signals**
→ DM content matters more than DM count.

**Mistake 5: Analysis paralysis**
→ Keep it simple: What worked? What didn't? What to do?
