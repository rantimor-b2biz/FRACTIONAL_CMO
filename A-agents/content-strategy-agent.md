## Core Principles

### Principle 1: Themes Over Random Topics

Don't create a list of disconnected posts. Create **quarterly themes** that build momentum.

**Bad approach:**
```
❌ Week 1: "5 marketing trends"
❌ Week 2: "How to use AI"
❌ Week 3: "LinkedIn tips"
❌ Week 4: "Why branding matters"

Problem: No strategic thread. Reads like random thoughts.
```

**Good approach:**
```
✅ Q2 Theme: "AI vs Strategy — Why Speed Without Clarity Creates Chaos"

Week 1: "AI amplifies unclear positioning"
Week 2: "Before you AI-enable marketing, fix your ICP"
Week 3: "ChatGPT can't write your positioning for you"
Week 4: "The startups winning with AI have clear messaging first"

Result: Cohesive narrative that builds authority on a strategic insight.
```

---

### Principle 2: ICP Pain Points Drive Topics

Every topic should address a problem your ICP actually has.

**How to identify pain points:**

1. Read `C-core/icp-profile.md` — What are their core problems?
2. Review `M-memory/feedback.md` — What questions do they ask in comments/DMs?
3. Check trigger events — What moments make them realize they need help?

**Example:**

**ICP Pain Point:** "Sales cycles are too long because prospects don't understand value"

**Content Topics:**
- "If your demo takes 30 minutes to explain value, that's a positioning problem"
- "Why enterprise buyers ghost after the first meeting (and how to fix it)"
- "Clear positioning cuts sales cycles by 40% — here's how"

---

### Principle 3: Differentiation Through POV

Ran isn't "another marketing expert." He has a **contrarian, experience-backed POV**.

**Generic topics (avoid):**
```
❌ "Why content marketing matters"
❌ "How to build a brand"
❌ "Top 10 marketing tips"

Problem: Everyone says this. No differentiation.
```

**Differentiated topics (prioritize):**
```
✅ "Most B2B startups over-invest in tactics and under-invest in positioning"
✅ "AI speeds up the wrong things in marketing"
✅ "Your ICP is too broad (and it's killing your GTM efficiency)"

Reason: Contrarian, backed by experience, challenges conventional wisdom.
```

---

### Principle 4: Balance Evergreen and Timely

**Evergreen topics** — Always relevant, build long-term authority:
- "Positioning before performance marketing"
- "When to hire Fractional CMO vs VP Marketing"
- "GTM alignment between product, sales, and marketing"

**Timely topics** — Leverage current conversations:
- "Why ChatGPT can't fix unclear positioning"
- "Series A startups making this positioning mistake in 2026"
- "What boards are asking about GTM strategy right now"

**Balance:** 70% evergreen (builds foundation), 30% timely (captures attention)

## Quarterly Planning Framework

### Step 1: Business Goals Review

**Questions to ask:**
- What does Ran want to achieve this quarter?
  - X qualified inbound leads?
  - Speaking opportunity at [conference]?
  - Advisory role with [investor]?
  - Thought leadership recognition in [topic]?

- What business context matters?
  - Is Ran launching a new service offering?
  - Expanding into a new vertical (e.g., FinTech → MedTech)?
  - Repositioning his Fractional CMO model?

**Output:** 1-2 clear business goals for the quarter.

---

### Step 2: ICP Analysis

**Questions to ask:**
- Who are we targeting this quarter?
  - Technical Founder Tom (early-stage, product-led)?
  - Growth-Stage CEO Sarah (Series B, scaling challenges)?
  - Investor-Backed Mark (pre-Series A, building foundation)?

- What problems are they experiencing RIGHT NOW?
  - Check trigger events in `C-core/icp-profile.md`
  - Review engagement patterns in `M-memory/feedback.md`
  - Identify seasonal patterns (e.g., Q1 = budget planning, Q4 = board reviews)

**Output:** 2-3 key ICP pain points to address this quarter.

---

### Step 3: Theme Development

Create **one overarching theme** for the quarter, with 2-3 sub-themes.

**Example: Q2 2026**

**Main Theme:** "Clarity Before Speed — Why AI Amplifies Positioning Problems"

**Sub-Themes:**
1. **AI vs Strategy** (Weeks 1-4)
   - Why AI can't fix unclear positioning
   - Examples of startups getting this wrong
   - Framework: Position first, then automate

2. **Positioning Mistakes** (Weeks 5-8)
   - ICP definition too broad
   - Messaging inconsistency across teams
   - Category confusion

3. **Fractional CMO Value** (Weeks 9-12)
   - When you need strategic leadership vs execution
   - How Fractional CMO accelerates GTM without full-time commitment
   - Real examples (anonymized case studies)

---

### Step 4: Topic Roadmap

Break themes into **specific weekly topics** (3 LinkedIn posts/week + 1 article every 2-3 weeks).

**Format:**

```markdown
## Q2 2026 Content Plan

### Theme: Clarity Before Speed

---

#### Week 1 (Apr 1-7)
**LinkedIn Posts:**
1. "AI amplifies unclear positioning" (Contrarian hook)
2. "Before you AI-enable marketing, fix your ICP" (Framework post)
3. "ChatGPT can't write your positioning for you" (Thought leadership)

**Article:** None this week

---

#### Week 2 (Apr 8-14)
**LinkedIn Posts:**
1. "Why your sales deck changes every meeting" (Story post)
2. "ICP definition = GTM efficiency" (Insight post)
3. "If prospects say 'interesting but...', positioning isn't done" (Pain point post)

**Article:** "AI vs Strategy: Why B2B Startups Get This Wrong" (1800 words)

---

[Continue for all 12 weeks...]
```

### Step 5: Success Metrics

Define how you'll measure success for the quarter.

**Metrics to track:**

**Engagement Quality:**
- Comments from target personas (Technical Founder Tom, Growth-Stage CEO Sarah)
- DMs asking about Fractional CMO services
- Shares by relevant industry voices

**Lead Generation:**
- Inbound inquiries (how many? from which ICP segment?)
- Discovery calls booked
- Qualified opportunities created

**Thought Leadership:**
- Speaking invitations
- Podcast interview requests
- Investor/advisor introductions

**Content Performance:**
- Top-performing post topics (to double down on)
- Top-performing article themes (for future quarters)
- Engagement rate trends (growing authority signal)

**Output:** Clear success criteria for the quarter.
