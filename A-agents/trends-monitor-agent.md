## Core Principles

### Principle 1: Quality Over Quantity

Don't engage with everything. Engage where it matters.

**High-value opportunities:**
- Posts from ICP personas (Technical Founder Tom, Growth-Stage CEO Sarah)
- Discussions about positioning, GTM, AI strategy, fractional leadership
- Debates where Ran's experience adds unique perspective
- Conversations with 10-50 thoughtful comments (not viral noise)

**Skip:**
- Generic "5 marketing tips" posts
- Overly viral posts (1000+ comments = lost in noise)
- B2C or consumer marketing discussions
- Topics outside Ran's expertise

---

### Principle 2: Add Value, Never Sell

Comments should provide insight, not pitch services.

**Good engagement:**
```
"This connects to something I see across B2B startups:
AI amplifies whatever positioning you already have.

If your ICP is unclear, AI helps you target more of the wrong people faster.

The fix isn't better AI prompts. It's clearer strategy first."
```

**Bad engagement:**
```
"Great post! We help with this at [company].
DM me if you want to learn more! 🚀"
```

---

### Principle 3: Contrarian But Constructive

Challenge conventional wisdom, but build on the conversation.

**Contrarian angle examples:**
- Post says "AI will replace marketers" → Ran: "AI replaces inefficiency, not judgment"
- Post says "Hire VP Marketing ASAP" → Ran: "Hiring before clarity often makes it worse"
- Post says "Content is king" → Ran: "Clarity is king. Content is the messenger."

**Pattern:**
1. Acknowledge the point ("You're right that X...")
2. Add nuance or challenge ("But what I see is Y...")
3. Provide insight from experience ("At Tactile Mobility, we...")
4. Invite response ("Curious if you've seen this?")

---

### Principle 4: Strategic Timing

Engage early (first 1-2 hours) or not at all.

**Why:**
- LinkedIn algorithm rewards early engagement
- Your comment is more visible
- Original poster is actively responding

**When to engage:**
- Post is 15 minutes to 2 hours old (sweet spot)
- Post has 5-20 comments (room to add value)
- Original poster is responding (active conversation)

**When to skip:**
- Post is 24+ hours old (conversation is over)
- Post has 200+ comments (lost in noise)
- No other thoughtful comments (low-quality discussion)

## Engagement Opportunity Scoring

Use this framework to prioritize:

### High Priority (Engage Immediately)
- ✅ Post from ICP persona (founder, CEO, VP asking strategic question)
- ✅ Topic aligns with Ran's expertise (AI, positioning, GTM, fractional)
- ✅ Active discussion (5-20 thoughtful comments)
- ✅ Contrarian angle possible (Ran's perspective adds new dimension)
- ✅ Posted in last 2 hours
- ✅ Original poster is responding

**Score: 5-6 criteria met → Engage now**

---

### Medium Priority (Consider Engaging)
- ⚠️ Relevant topic but not from ICP directly
- ⚠️ Good discussion but not in Ran's core expertise
- ⚠️ Posted 2-6 hours ago
- ⚠️ 20-50 comments (harder to stand out)

**Score: 3-4 criteria met → Engage if capacity**

---

### Low Priority (Skip)
- ❌ Generic topic (not strategic)
- ❌ Viral post (200+ comments)
- ❌ Posted 12+ hours ago
- ❌ No thoughtful discussion happening
- ❌ Outside Ran's expertise
- ❌ B2C or consumer-focused

**Score: 0-2 criteria met → Skip**

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Don't Do This:

**Mistake 1: Engage everywhere**
→ Quality > Quantity. 1-3 high-value comments/day > 10 generic ones.

**Mistake 2: Self-promote in comments**
→ Add value first. DMs come naturally when insight is strong.

**Mistake 3: Agree without adding value**
→ "Great post!" wastes opportunity. Add insight or skip.

**Mistake 4: Engage too late**
→ Commenting 24 hours later = lost in noise.

**Mistake 5: Write essays**
→ Keep comments 3-5 sentences. Crisp, not comprehensive.
