## Writing Principles

### Principle 1: Executive Voice, Not Marketing Fluff

Ran speaks at the CEO/Board level. Not fluffy, not salesy, not generic.

**Bad (Marketing Fluff):**
```
❌ "Are you ready to revolutionize your go-to-market strategy with cutting-edge AI?
Let's unlock your potential! 🚀"
```

**Good (Executive Voice):**
```
✅ "AI amplifies what you feed it.

If your positioning is unclear, AI just makes the confusion faster.

Fix the foundation first."
```

---

### Principle 2: One Clear Insight Per Post

Don't try to say everything. One idea, well delivered.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** — Contrarian statement or bold claim (first 2 lines)
2. **Context** — Why this matters
3. **Insight** — The strategic take
4. **Application** — What to do about it
5. **CTA** — Question for engagement

**Example:**
```
Your ICP is too broad. [Hook]

When I ask founders "who do you serve?" they say "any company with [problem]." [Context]

That's not an ICP. That's a TAM. [Insight]

A real ICP narrows down to:
• Company size (20-200 employees)
• Funding stage (Seed to Series B)
• Specific industry (FinTech, MedTech, etc.)
• Clear trigger event (just raised funding, entering new market)

Narrow your ICP, focus your GTM. [Application]

How narrow is your ICP definition? [CTA]
```

---

### Principle 3: Contrarian But Not Combative

Challenge conventional wisdom, but with evidence, not just hot takes.

**Bad (Combative):**
```
❌ "Everyone doing performance marketing is an idiot. You're wasting money."
```

**Good (Contrarian with Evidence):**
```
✅ "Most B2B startups launch performance marketing before positioning is clear.

Result? High spend, low conversion.

At Tactile Mobility, we repositioned first, then ran campaigns. Sales cycles dropped 40%."
```

---

### Principle 4: Specific Over Vague

Numbers, examples, concrete details. No generic claims.

**Vague:**
```
❌ "Clear positioning improves sales performance significantly."
```

**Specific:**
```
✅ "Clear positioning cut our sales cycles from 12 months to 7 months."
```

---

### Principle 5: Format for Mobile

Short lines. White space. Easy to scan.

**Bad:**
```
❌ One long paragraph that goes on and on without breaks making it hard to read on mobile and people will just scroll past because it looks like work.
```

**Good:**
```
✅ Short lines.

White space.

Easy to scan.

One idea per line.
```

## Hook Formulas (for B2B Executive Voice)

### Hook 1: Contrarian Statement
```
"AI speeds up the wrong things."
"Your ICP is too broad."
"Positioning beats performance marketing."
```

### Hook 2: Bold Observation
```
"I see B2B startups making the same positioning mistake."
"Most pitch decks fail in the first 30 seconds."
"Your sales team shouldn't need 30 minutes to explain value."
```

### Hook 3: Specific Story
```
"Last week, a Series B CEO showed me their deck.

It changed 5 times in the last month."
```

### Hook 4: Provocative Question
```
"Why does your pitch deck change after every sales meeting?"
"If AI is so powerful, why is marketing getting noisier?"
```

### Hook 5: Numbers That Surprise
```
"Sales cycles dropped 40% after we fixed positioning.

Not because we sold harder. Because we positioned clearer."
```

## Post Structures

### Structure 1: Contrarian Take
```
[Contrarian hook]

[Why most people get this wrong]

[The real insight]

[What to do instead]

[CTA question]
```

### Structure 2: Framework Post
```
[Problem statement]

Here's the [X]-step framework:

1. [Step] — [Brief explanation]
2. [Step] — [Brief explanation]
3. [Step] — [Brief explanation]

[Closing insight]

[CTA]
```

### Structure 3: Before/After
```
[Hook about the problem]

Before:
• [Pain point]
• [Pain point]
• [Pain point]

After:
• [Result]
• [Result]
• [Result]

The shift? [One key insight]

[CTA]
```

### Structure 4: Story → Lesson
```
[Specific story opening]

[Context]

[What happened]

[Lesson learned]

[How others can apply]

[CTA]
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Don't Write Like This:

**Mistake 1: Corporate Jargon**
```
"Leverage synergistic capabilities to optimize your go-to-market infrastructure."
```

**Mistake 2: Hype Language**
```
"Revolutionary AI-powered solution that will transform your business! 🚀"
```

**Mistake 3: Vague Claims**
```
"Marketing is changing. You need to adapt."
```

**Mistake 4: Too Long**
```
[500-word essay without line breaks]
```

**Mistake 5: Generic Advice**
```
"Here are 5 marketing trends you need to know."
```
