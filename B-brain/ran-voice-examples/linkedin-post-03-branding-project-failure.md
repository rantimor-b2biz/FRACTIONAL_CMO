## The Post

"The project failed."
"You didn't deliver."

If you run a marketing agency, you have probably heard this before.

+ The kickoff was strong.
+ The thinking was solid.
+ The work was done.

And yet the result "didn't land".

In most cases, the agency was not the problem.

What failed was the structure around the project.
- No internal owner of marketing decisions.
- No one defining what success actually looks like.
- No strategic leadership shaping goals, refining outcomes, and aligning both sides.

Agencies are asked to execute while also deciding what matters.
That is not execution. That is absorbing organizational confusion.

In my latest article, I break down why branding projects fail without ownership, and why fractional CMOs play a critical role not just connecting sides, but setting direction, refining results, and extracting the best from both the company and the agency.

Question for fellow agency owners and marketing leaders:
How often are you brought into branding or strategy projects without a clear internal owner or defined outcomes, and how do you handle it when you recognize this early?
