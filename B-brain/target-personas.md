# Target Personas

> Detailed profiles of our ideal customer segments.

---

## Persona 1: Technical Founder Tom

**Profile:**
- Co-Founder & CEO of Series A B2B SaaS startup
- 35 employees, raised $8M
- FinTech / Cybersecurity / Data Infrastructure background
- Product-minded, technical background

**Company Stage:**
- Product works, early enterprise customers acquired
- Founder-led sales still closing most deals
- No marketing leadership - maybe junior marketer running campaigns

**Pain Points:**
- Sales cycle is 9-12 months (too long)
- Messaging changes every pitch
- Website built by product team ("it's fine, but...")
- Board asking: "When are you hiring a VP Marketing?"

**Trigger Events:**
- Recent funding round
- Board pressure for GTM acceleration
- Lost deal to better-positioned competitor
- Entering enterprise segment

**Content That Resonates:**
- "If your sales demo takes 30 minutes to explain value, positioning isn't done"
- "Sales cycles dropped 40% after we fixed positioning"
- "ICP definition = GTM efficiency"

**Engagement Behavior:**
- Active on LinkedIn (reads during commute)
- Engages with strategic content (not tactics)
- Reaches out via DM when facing specific GTM challenge

## Persona 2: Growth-Stage CEO Sarah

**Profile:**
- CEO of Series B/C MedTech or Enterprise SaaS startup
- 120 employees, raised $30M
- Non-technical background, commercial/business focus
- Experienced operator (this is not her first rodeo)

**Company Stage:**
- Expanding internationally (US → Europe or vice versa)
- Competing against better-funded, better-positioned companies
- Head of Marketing runs campaigns, but no strategic ownership
- Considering VP Marketing hire but unsure what to look for

**Pain Points:**
- Marketing produces content, but sales says "it doesn't help"
- Losing deals to competitors with clearer positioning
- Board wants faster pipeline growth
- Current messaging doesn't differentiate

**Trigger Events:**
- Lost 3 major deals to same competitor
- Investor feedback: "Get your messaging right before you scale"
- Entering new vertical or geography
- Preparing for Series C raise

**Content That Resonates:**
- "Most B2B startups over-invest in tactics, under-invest in positioning"
- "AI amplifies confusion when messaging isn't clear"
- "Fractional CMO vs VP Marketing: When to use which model"

**Engagement Behavior:**
- Strategic reader (wants frameworks, not tips)
- Shares content internally with leadership team
- Books discovery calls after reading 2-3 articles

## Persona 3: Investor-Backed Mark

**Profile:**
- Founder & CEO of Seed-stage Cybersecurity or AI Infrastructure startup
- 25 employees, raised $4M
- Strong technical product, early customer traction
- Founder still doing all sales

**Company Stage:**
- Product works, 3-5 customers validating need
- Building pipeline but struggling with conversion
- No marketing function (founder doing everything)
- Preparing for Series A in 6-12 months

**Pain Points:**
- Prospects say "interesting, but we don't understand how this fits"
- ICP is too broad ("any company with security needs")
- Website doesn't convert traffic
- Investor said: "You need marketing leadership before Series A"

**Trigger Events:**
- Investor pushback on GTM readiness
- Struggling to articulate value in investor meetings
- Lost deal because buyer didn't understand positioning
- Hiring first marketing person (unsure where to start)

**Content That Resonates:**
- "Your ICP is too broad (and it's killing your GTM efficiency)"
- "When to hire Fractional CMO vs VP Marketing"
- "Positioning before performance marketing"

**Engagement Behavior:**
- Early in awareness (learning about marketing leadership)
- Follows for education (not ready to buy immediately)
- Reaches out 3-6 months after initial engagement
